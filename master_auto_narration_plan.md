# Master Auto-Narration & Feedback Plan

## 1. Current State Assessment
Currently, `AppState.swift` handles both Proactive Narration and Post-Activity Feedback using **continuous background GPS polling** (every few seconds). 
- **The Problem:** 
  1. *Battery Death:* Polling GPS constantly in the background will destroy the user's battery and cause iOS to terminate the app.
  2. *Naive Intelligence:* The current POI scoring is a static math formula (`preferenceMatchScore * 0.6 + distanceScore * 0.4`). It doesn't care if it's 2 AM (and suggests a museum) or if the user just heard 4 facts about historical buildings in a row (fatigue).

## 2. Objective Best Architecture: The Implementation Plan

To build a world-class AI tour guide, we must decouple this from `AppState` and utilize native iOS efficiency alongside dynamic algorithmic scoring.

### Step 1: The Dynamic POI Scoring Engine (Supabase Edge Function)
Forget static weights calculated on the iPhone. We need a dynamically weighted algorithm integrating 4 context vectors, executed entirely on the backend via a **Supabase Edge Function** (`/rank-pois`) so the iPhone doesn't melt trying to calculate millions of PostGIS coordinates.

* **Score Formula:** `FinalScore = Σ(w_i * Factor_i)` or a weighted product model across the following vectors:
  1. **Spatial Proximity:** Use a Gaussian decay function. (e.g., 1.0 at 10m, exponentially dropping to 0.1 at 200m).
  2. **Personalization (Long-Term):** Base multipliers on the user's historical profile (e.g., if they are an art lover, `w2 = 1.5` for Museums).
  3. **Recency Fatigue (Short-Term):** *This is crucial.* If the AI just narrated a "Heritage" site 2 minutes ago, the weight for all other Heritage sites drops to `0.2`. It forces the AI to vary its topics instead of spamming similar facts.
  4. **Temporal Context:** Time-gates. A café gets a `1.5` multiplier at 8:00 AM, but a `0.1` multiplier at 11:00 PM. 
  5. **Global Quality & Popularity:** Foursquare ratings/check-in density. A highly-rated landmark effortlessly outranks a generic 2-star diner.
  6. **Current Weather Context:** Integrated with `WeatherService`. If it is raining, penalize open-air parks and massively boost indoor venues (museums, cafes).
  7. **User Velocity & Transportation Mode:** If `CMMotionActivityManager` detects driving at 30mph, penalize small architectural details (they will breeze past them) and boost large sights. If walking, boost dense, pedestrian-friendly POIs.
  8. **Active Session Conversation Intent:** If the user just casually asked the AI about "local history", dynamically skyrocket the weight of historical POIs for the next 10 minutes. 
  9. **Semantic Fit (Intrinsic POI Knowledge Graph):** Using `pgvector` in the database, calculate cosine similarity between the User's Preference Vector and a pre-computed **Semantic POI Vector**. This POI Vector is generated by running an LLM over Foursquare reviews, menus, and amenity tags. It elevates recommendations from generic ("It's a coffee shop") to hyper-specific ("It's a quiet coffee shop with gluten-free options and a dog-friendly patio, perfectly matching your allergy and pet profile"). 

### Step 2: Dwell Time & Feedback Prompting (Zero Battery Drain)
We must completely abandon manual GPS polling for Dwell Time.

* **The iOS Geofence Strategy (The 19+1 Pagination Rule):**
  1. iOS provides `CoreLocation` Geofencing (`CLCircularRegion`), which uses ultra-low-power cell-tower triangulation to monitor areas in the background. Note: iOS strictly limits you to **20 monitored regions** at a time per app.
  2. **The Initial Load:** When the user starts a session, `ContextPipeline` executes the 9-Vector Math and Supabase returns the Top 19 most relevant POIs. *Crucially, these coordinates must be pulled passively from the Mapbox Stream, NOT from a new CLLocationManager, preserving the Zero Battery Drain guarantee.*
  3. Register these 19 POIs as `CLCircularRegion` geofences.
  4. **The Bounding Box (Region 20):** Register the 20th Geofence as a massive 2-kilometer wide "Master Bounding Box" centered on the user. If the user drives completely out of the neighborhood, they exit the bounding box, which triggers a background fetch for the *next* 19 POIs, solving the pagination trap.
  5. **The Dwell Logic (Background Audio Resumption):**
     - When iOS fires `didEnterRegion(region)`, save the timestamp locally (`UserDefaults`).
     - When iOS fires `didExitRegion(region)` in the deep background (meaning the user's phone is asleep in their pocket), subtract the timestamps. 
     - If `timeSpent > 10 minutes`, the app *cannot* just "send a text string" to ElevenLabs because the WebRTC socket might be dormant. Instead, it must trigger the **Background Audio Resumption Protocol**. `AudioSessionManager` aggressively claims `AVAudioSession` priority, re-initializes LiveKit WebRTC, and uses ElevenLabs' `Client Event / Add Context` API to dynamically inject a silent system prompt: *"The user spent 15 mins at {Region.name}. Ask them about it out loud."*
  6. **Refresh:** As the user moves significantly and breaks the Bounding Box, unregister old regions and register the next 19 closest high-score POIs along with a new Bounding Box.

## 3. Anticipated Problems & Fixes
- **Problem:** Geofence transitions (Entering/Exiting) are not perfectly instantaneous; iOS delays them to prevent jitter.
  - **Fix:** Do not rely on Geofences for instantaneous turn-by-turn navigation (we use Mapbox for that). Geofences are strictly for Dwell Time (which inherently doesn't need to be down-to-the-second accurate).
- **Problem:** The AI interrupts the user constantly because too many POIs score high.
  - **Fix:** Implement a global `Token Bucket` rate-limiter in the Cloud Engine. The AI earns 1 "Narration Token" every 3 minutes. If the bucket is empty, it stays silent even if passing the Eiffel Tower. 

## 4. Competitive Differentiators (Lessons from Top Products)
To definitively outclass current market leaders (like Autoura, VoiceMap, or GuideGeek), we can incorporate these final advanced features into the engine:

1. **True Spatial Audio (Directional Voice):** Instead of standard stereo, we can use iOS `AVAudioEnvironmentNode` to pan the ElevenLabs audio stream in 3D space. If the AI says, *"Look at that cathedral to your right"*, the audio will literally sound like the AI is standing to the user's right (especially immersive with AirPods Pro Head Tracking).
2. **Hybrid Offline Fallback (The Tunnel Fix):** Top GPS audio tours (like VoiceMap) pre-cache audio. Our app should pre-fetch the semantic data for the next 1km and cache it locally. If the user walks into a dead zone and the LiveKit WebRTC Socket drops, the Geofence still fires locally and gracefully degrades to Apple's on-device `AVSpeechSynthesizer` to narrate the POI until LTE is restored.
3. **Information Density Tracking (Audio Pacing):** Beyond a simple token bucket (rate limiter), the engine tracks the total *word count* spoken in the last 15 minutes. High-end museum guides ensure "golden silence" so users can actually enjoy looking at the building. The AI analyzes not just *how often* it spoke, but *how dense* the information was, naturally expanding the silence gap after a long explanation.

---
**Summary:** By shifting from manual GPS polling to native iOS Geofencing, we save battery. By shifting from static math to a dynamic, recency-aware scoring algorithm, the AI sounds vastly more intelligent and context-aware.
